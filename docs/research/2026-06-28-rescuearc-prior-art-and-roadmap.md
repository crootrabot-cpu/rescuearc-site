# RescueArc Prior-Art and Commercialization Note

## Blunt thesis
The broad lane — throwable flotation that inflates after water entry — is probably too crowded to own cleanly. The real wedge is narrower: a rescue device that average people can throw farther and more accurately than a ring buoy, with safe deployment and actually useful flotation once it lands.

## Directional prior-art read
Closest adjacent lanes:
- ring buoys / horseshoe buoys
- throw bags / rescue lines
- rescue tubes and cans
- compact self-inflating throwable flotation devices
- inflatable buoy safety systems
- powered remote rescue buoys

Implication:
- broad utility claim confidence: low to medium
- narrow mechanism / workflow / geometry claim confidence: better

## Best claim wedges to investigate
- aerodynamic throwable rescue geometry optimized for longer / more accurate throws
- rackable lifeguard workflow with repeated throw attempts
- safe-impact shell plus post-impact inflation sequence
- water-trigger and backup-delay deployment logic
- inflated geometry that self-orients or remains easy to grab in panic conditions

## Biggest technical risks
- projectile hardness / impact safety
- accidental deployment
- no-fire or partial inflation
- inflated form not actually helping the victim
- corrosive marine storage degrading reliability

## What must be proven before taking money
- throw distance improvement vs ring buoy baseline
- throw accuracy improvement across average users
- safe impact profile
- deployment reliability
- victim usability of the inflated form
- rough cost structure and re-arm economics

## Recommended sequence
1. invention disclosure and embodiment map
2. attorney-grade prior-art review
3. prototype architecture and BOM assumptions
4. bench testing
5. comparative throw study
6. supplier / compliance / MOQ discovery
7. pilot market validation
8. only then crowdfunding or reservations

## Vendor categories likely needed
- industrial design / product engineering
- soft-goods or inflatable fabricator
- inflator / cartridge integration specialist
- electrical / trigger consultant if electronic backup logic is used
- prototype machine shop / CAD support
- testing / compliance lab
- sourcing partner

## Best founder narrative
Not: “It inflates in water.”

Instead:
- “We redesigned the first rescue throw.”
- “The old standard is hard to throw. Ours is easier to launch and faster to reach.”
- “Swim-out second. Better throw first.”
