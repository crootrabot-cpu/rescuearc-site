The story

Don’t start with the swim. Start with the throw.

Most public rescue hardware still asks the responder to make an awkward first toss with a shape that was never designed to fly well. Life Launch is built around the opposite idea: make the first move cleaner, earlier, and easier to repeat from the station.

The point is not gadget theater. The point is a more credible first-response sequence for aquatic teams that need range, visibility, and faster contact.

How the system works

Mounted ready. Thrown fast. Seen easily.

  1. Stage it at elevation: keep units mounted where the responder already watches the water.
  2. Throw from a cleaner form factor: the compact body is meant to read closer to a trained athletic motion than a bulky ring toss.
  3. Deploy support after impact: visible flotation becomes the victim’s first support while the responder closes distance.
