Product read

A compact rescue body designed to throw cleaner than legacy gear.

The close-up makes the product legible fast: rescue-orange shell, black grip surfaces, directional body geometry, and stabilizing fins that give it a real marine-equipment feel.

That visual matters. If the object looks vague or toy-like, the whole concept collapses. Here it reads like a serious throwable device meant for range, control, and fast recognition.

How the system works

Mounted at the chair. Released in seconds. Repeated if needed.

  1. Stage it where the eyes already are: the unit lives at the elevated tower or platform where the responder is already scanning the water line.
  2. Grab and release without a setup dance: the device is intended to support a cleaner overhand throwing motion than bulky ring-based gear.
  3. Keep repeated attempts within reach: the rack matters because a missed first throw should not end the tool’s usefulness.
  4. Create flotation sooner: the target outcome is faster support in the water while the human rescue response keeps moving.
