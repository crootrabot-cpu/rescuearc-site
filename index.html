Product reveal

A more throwable rescue object, not another awkward ring.

RescueArc starts with the launch problem. The compact pre-deployment body is meant to feel closer to a trained athletic throw than a bulky ring-buoy toss, while still reading as serious marine rescue hardware instead of a toy.

  • Compact, directional throw form for faster first contact attempts.
  • Premium industrial cues: fins, grip surfaces, seams, and marine-grade materials.
  • Purpose-built to become flotation after impact rather than stay a clumsy projectile.

System story

The wedge is the station, not just the object.

RescueArc becomes strategically interesting when it lives on a chair, station, or patrol position with repeated-shot access. The first rescue move gets faster because the responder stays elevated, grabs instantly, and throws before a long swim-out becomes the only option.

  1. Scan from elevation: keep the responder in position instead of committing immediately to the water.
  2. Grab from the rack: mounted access keeps the device visible and ready.
  3. Throw again fast if needed: this is a repeated-shot system story, not one desperate toss.
  4. Swim-out second: flotation reaches the victim earlier while the responder closes the gap.
